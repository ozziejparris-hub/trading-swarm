## Task 3 — N=2 Events: Power Analysis

### Assessment

**N=2 distinct events is INSUFFICIENT for generalisable claims.**

1. **Cannot estimate between-event variance.** The MWU p-value tests within-sample variation across the combined pool. With n=2 events, we cannot determine whether the (pooled) signal generalises to other geopolitics events or is specific to the Haley/Iran Nov-Dec 2025 context.

2. **Events are temporally clustered, not independent.** Both events resolved within 12 days of each other (Haley: Nov 20, Iran: Dec 2, 2025). Both involved Middle East / US political themes. Trader overlap is possible — some Iran candidates may have also monitored the Haley market. These are not two truly independent data points.

3. **Sample sizes insufficient for event-level tests.** Haley clean: n=36 candidates vs n=53 control. Iran clean: n=23 vs n=37. At these sample sizes and observed effect size r≈0.15, statistical power is approximately 25-35% per event. We would EXPECT to fail event-level significance even if the true effect is real.

4. **Power calculation — combined test:**
   - Observed effect size r=0.208 (rank-biserial)
   - Combined n=59 candidates, n=90 control
   - Estimated power: ~69%
   - For 80% power at this effect size: need ~100 candidates per event
   - We have 36 (Haley) and 23 (Iran) — well below threshold

5. **Minimum requirement for deployment as trading signal: 5 additional independent events** across different political contexts, time periods, and market structures. Current n=2 (in same 12-day window) is insufficient.

**Verdict on Task 3:** N=2 EVENTS INSUFFICIENT. Cannot distinguish "real but small signal" from "coincidental result in small sample." Statistical power is below 70% for the combined test and ~30% per event.

## Task 4 — Iran Market: Include or Exclude?

### Background

The market "Will Iran recognize Iran by June 2025?" has a provably impossible self-referential title. Most likely interpretation: "Will Iran recognize Israel?" (consistent with late-2025 geopolitics). Resolution: "No."

ELO clustering concern: multiple Iran candidates show ELO at exactly 3000.0, 2700.0, 2000.0 — round-number values consistent with accounts that received ELO from a single resolved trade (starting at 1500 base, boosted by the win). 7 Iran candidates are flagged THIN_SAMPLE_ARTIFACT.

### Statistical Results

| Pooled WITH Iran | 59 | 90 | 0.0160 | Yes (pooled) |
| Pooled WITHOUT Iran | 36 | 53 | 0.1087 | No |

**Key finding: Removing Iran does NOT produce a stronger signal — it makes the pooled result WEAKER (Haley-only p=0.1087).**

The earlier v1 claim that "signal is STRONGER without Iran" was generated by the confounded cross-event comparison (Haley vs ALL control). In the correct event-specific comparison, Iran exclusion actually removes the weaker-but-still-directional component and leaves only the non-significant Haley result.

### Recommendation

**EXCLUDE Iran from primary analysis (data quality concern: impossible title), but note that excluding Iran does NOT strengthen the remaining signal.** The statistical case for the lifecycle heuristic depends on the pooled sample, not on either event alone.

**Verdict on Task 4:** EXCLUDE Iran (data quality). But the effect of exclusion is to REDUCE statistical confidence, not increase it. The heuristic needs more clean events, not fewer.
