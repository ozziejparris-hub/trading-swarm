# When Do Markets Fully Process Public Information? Evidence from Real-Time Prediction Markets
## Source
https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07811
## Domain
arxiv.org
## Summary
Uses real-time binary prediction markets with precisely observed public signals and high-frequency data to study belief updating efficiency. Finds prices respond rapidly and in the correct direction to public signals, but directional responsiveness is not the same as efficient updating — mispricing windows persist post-signal. Directly relevant to RQ1.1 (ELO persistence) and the system's core premise of exploitable mispricings.
